The Greatest Quotes

"I can think of nothing less pleasurable than a life devoted to pleasure."

- John D. Rockefeller

Vocabulary

  • Pleasurable: Enjoyable, pleasant.
  • Devoted: Giving all or a large part of one's time or resources to a person, activity, or cause.
  • Pleasure: A feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment.
  • Think: Have a particular opinion, belief, or idea about someone or something.
  • Life: The existence of an individual human being or animal.
  • Nothing: Not anything, no single thing.
  • Less: A smaller amount of something.
  • Devotion: Love, loyalty, or enthusiasm for a person, activity, or cause.
  • Satisfaction: Fulfillment of one's wishes, expectations, or needs.
  • Enjoyment: The state or process of taking pleasure in something.

Round One: Yes/No Questions

  1. Does the author believe a life of pleasure is good?
  2. Is the quote about the importance of hard work?
  3. Does the author value pleasure?
  4. Is the quote from a famous philosopher?
  5. Does the quote suggest that a life of constant fun is unfulfilling?
  6. Is the author promoting a simple life?
  7. Is the word 'devoted' used in a positive way here?
  8. Is this a modern quote?
  9. Does the quote imply that there are more important things than pleasure?
  10. Could this quote be interpreted as a paradox?

Round Two: Agree/Disagree Statements

  1. A life devoted to pleasure is the best kind of life.
  2. True happiness comes from purpose, not from pleasure.
  3. John D. Rockefeller was right about pleasure.
  4. This quote is too simplistic.
  5. Modern society is too focused on pleasure.
  6. It is impossible to be 'devoted' to pleasure.
  7. This quote is inspiring.
  8. People who seek pleasure all the time are usually unhappy.
  9. The pursuit of pleasure is a valid life goal.
  10. This quote applies to everyone.
