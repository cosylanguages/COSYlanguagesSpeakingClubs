Keeping Up With Science

Unprecedented Climate Shocks Are Changing the Great Lakes Forever

Extreme heat waves and cold spells on the Great Lakes have more than doubled since the late 1990s, coinciding with a major El Niño event. Using advanced ocean-style modeling adapted for the lakes, researchers traced temperature trends back to 1940.

Vocabulary

  • Unprecedented: Never done or known before.
  • Climate: The weather conditions prevailing in an area in general or over a long period.
  • Shocks: Sudden, unexpected, and usually unpleasant events or experiences.
  • Coinciding: Occurring at or during the same time.
  • Modeling: The use of mathematical models to simulate real-life situations.
  • Extreme: Reaching a high or the highest degree; very great.
  • Spells: A short period of a particular type of weather.
  • Trends: A general direction in which something is developing or changing.
  • Adapted: Made suitable for a new use or purpose; modified.
  • Traced: Found or discovered by investigation.

Round One: Yes/No Questions

  1. Have extreme weather events on the Great Lakes decreased?
  2. Did the study use old methods for modeling?
  3. Is this study about the oceans?
  4. Did the researchers look at temperature trends from the last 10 years only?
  5. Was the El Niño event a minor factor?
  6. Is the article about the effects of pollution?
  7. Is the term 'unprecedented' used to mean 'common'?
  8. Are the Great Lakes located in Europe?
  9. Did the study find that the number of extreme weather events has stayed the same?
  10. Does the summary mention anything about fish?

Round Two: Agree/Disagree Statements

  1. Climate change is not a serious issue for the Great Lakes.
  2. Computer modeling is a reliable way to study climate trends.
  3. The El Niño event is the only cause of the changes in the Great Lakes.
  4. We should not be concerned about these climate shocks.
  5. This research is important for understanding the future of the Great Lakes.
  6. The summary provides a complete picture of the research.
  7. The vocabulary used in the summary is too complex for the general public.
  8. More research is needed to confirm these findings.
  9. The government should take action based on this research.
  10. This kind of research is a waste of money.
